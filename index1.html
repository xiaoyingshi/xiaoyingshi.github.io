Abstract
What is SELINA ?

Single-cell RNA-seq has emerged as a powerful technique for profiling heterogeneous cell populations and tracking cellular dynamics in unprecedented resolution. With the massive accumulation of single-cell RNA-seq data, an efficient and accurate computational method for cell-type annotation is urgently needed. Here, we present SELINA (Single cELl Identity NAvigator), a tool combined reference-based and marker-based methods for automatic cell-type annotation at both single-cell and cluster levels. SELINA creates a curated reference map based on cell ontology and marker-dependent annotations from 303 public single-cell RNA-seq datasets across 66 tissues, covering 331 major human cell types. Using this extensive reference map, SELINA applies an ensemble learning-based method to annotate the data automatically. We demonstrate the effectiveness of SELINA on both single-cell benchmark datasets with or without known cell-type labels.

In summary, SELINA builds the computational framework with a comprehensive and extensible reference map for automatic cell-type annotation, facilitating the broad application of single-cell classification.
